Introduction :

Teradata builds the best EDW. Teradata's shared nothing architecture, parallelism and linear scalability are the best in the industry. If your organization does not wan't to move to the cloud, Teradata is still the better option over Oracle, DB2, SQL server, Vertica and Greenplum.

The Teradata Quicksand:

Your company has spent thousands of man hours over the last two decades building a Best-in-Class Enterprise Data Warehouse. You employ an army of resources to maintain and manage your Data Warehouse systems: including Teradata Managed Services. You have a dedicated Teradata account team on-call to address your needs and provide you with the best user experience.

Your Teradata team has a solution for every problem:

  • Cost Reduction? Teradata Decoded, Data Mart Consolidation.
  • Database Best Practices? Stop exporting data, Analyze data where it lies, In-database processing.
  • Performance and Workload Management issues? TASM, PDCR, Secondary Indexes

    • Every two or three years Teradata provides you with a multi-million $ Floorsweep option, Database Upgrade, High-Availability DR, or a Business Value Roadmap.
    In early 2014, the narrative started to shift a bit. It was no longer just about structured transactional data. Business was demanding semi and unstructured data.
    Organizations were being put under pressure to explore alternatives, justify the ROI of operating a monolithic EDW and evaluate ways to offload workloads. Business continued to put pressure on IT on data latency and the type of data that was being ingested. During the "Hadoop Era", in response to CTO demands, Teradata was quick to manage the exodus by adopting Hadoop nodes in the same rack with Teradata nodes, all connected using the "BYNET" and releasing a Teradata-Hadoop connector with QueryGrid.

    All was well in San Diego, with the New Teradata Sentient Enterprise: A younger CIO with a clear vision: "CLOUD"

    Meanwhile, the industry was leapfrogging from this legacy EDW implementation to a cloud-centric modern architecture with greater control over managing consumption and cost.
    "All roads lead to Teradata". Account Executives focused on ARR to help drive growth of data, users and the platform addressing your concerns about ROI and TCO?

    "Is this the Teradata Quicksand?" Teradata needed to stay relevant as IT organizations started to stand up smaller systems to test real-time data ingestion and address latency with smaller workloads.Teradata addressed this shift with Vantage in the Cloud.

    With a DIY cloud option:Vantage on AWS, Azure and GCP, you can provision a base, advanced or enterprise tier system based on your existing configuration. This is a step in the right direction. But is it too little too late? Is Teradata Vantage similar to an on-premises system, just in the cloud?


    How do you build a cloud-first data lake? The requirements can be addressed by an EDW, but the solution comes with a high price tag:

    • Data Ingestion: All structured, semi-structured and unstructured
    • Data Storage: Find the right data placement strategy, read, write, update, static, etc.
    • Data Frequency: Batch, nightly, daily, all-day mini-batch, real-time, stream
    • Data Analytics: Adhoc, Canned, Self-service BI, Data Science, predictive, prescriptive
    • Scalability: Must be able to scale up or down based on demand
    • Cost and Consumption: Turn off ETL and business user access on weekends to stop all billing
    • Value: Spend less time preparing and more time Analyzing

      • Traditional EDW implementation:

      The EDW Architecture has it's limitations:

      • Not a Scalable Architecture, and despite what Teradata will tell you, it is not Linearly Scalable.
      • Ability to ingest only structured data.
      • Cost of Storing Data in the EDW is massive. In some cases over 20x.(even with compression)
      • Primarily batch-oriented, not real-time, and streaming is very expensive.
      • Business has access to stale data, and that's a fact.
      • It takes a long time to deploy and maintain. And changes are expensive.

      Q & A: Survival Tips

      Is there a way out, and how can DataLake Labs help ?

      The short answer is Yes. And, there is an EASY button. DataLake Labs will help you understand your Teradata Eco-system and migrate your database to Vantage on any-cloud. Once in the cloud, We will repoint your on-premise ETL to Vantage. Repoint your BI users to Vantage. After we have stabilized your system in the cloud, we will decommission your on-premise system. Certain workloads will fail to migrate, and that is a good thing. It's time for some basic housekeeping and let the failed processes fail. Once in the cloud, you now have the ability to manage your consumption. You can build the roadmap to further offload workloads to other less expensive platforms, and develop a schedule to scale down the system on weekends and holidays. This alone can save you over a million $ per year.

      Why move to Vantage in the Cloud? Isn't that more of the same?

      Your on-premsies Teradata system is tightly coupled with other ETL/ETL software and BI users. It is a daunting task to untangle the web. Movign to Vantgae first helps de-couple these processes. Vantge in the cloud could be an interim state or a final state. That really depends on your cloud strategy.

      What about my ETL and BI? Will I experience any latency?

      You can leave your ETL and BI on-premises as phase 1 and test latency and SLA's. If the Latency and performance is unacceptable, we can lift and shift your on-premises ETL server to the cloud.

      Is there a charge for Migrating to Vantage? Are you using a tool?

      Yes, there is a charge.And, no we are not leveraging a tool. but we are using an Archive and Restore at the database level to migrate you to the Cloud. This is the best and the fastest method with the least amount of disruption to the business

      What if i am on an older release of Teradata? How about 14.10?

      We can still use the same process of migrating. We will get warnings and errors. But no reason to panic. These warnings while true, are not going to prevent you from migrating. You just have to address them at some point.
